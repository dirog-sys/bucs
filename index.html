About

The 2002 Tampa Bay Buccaneers defense, led by defensive coordinator Monte Kiffin, was nothing short of legendary, earning them the moniker "Tampa 2" defense. Anchored by a formidable front seven, highlighted by defensive tackle Warren Sapp and linebacker Derrick Brooks, the Buccaneers boasted one of the most dominant defenses in the history of the NFL. Their defensive prowess was characterized by an aggressive pass rush, disruptive playmaking, and exceptional coverage in the secondary. The team's defensive backfield, featuring future Hall of Famer Ronde Barber and safety John Lynch, complemented the front seven seamlessly. This formidable unit played a crucial role in the Buccaneers' journey to Super Bowl XXXVII, where they secured a resounding victory over the Oakland Raiders, sealing their place in history as one of the most dominant defenses to ever grace the NFL.
